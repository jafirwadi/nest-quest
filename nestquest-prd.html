01 — Strategic Foundation

Why most real estate sites
fail to convert

Most real estate landing pages are digital brochures. They list properties, show floor plans, and ask visitors to "contact us." They ignore the fundamental truth: buying or renting a home is the most emotionally charged financial decision a person makes. Logic closes contracts, but emotion opens wallets.

This PRD is built around a single counter-strategy: lead with story, not inventory. The page should make visitors feel something before they see anything — and that feeling should be "this brand understands what I want."

◈

Core Thesis: We are not selling square footage. We are selling a future self — the version of the visitor who already lives in the home of their dreams. Every section must serve that story. Properties are evidence. Trust signals are proof. The CTA is the invitation.

The AIDA Conversion Arc

Each section of the page maps directly to a conversion psychology stage. No section is decorative — each one has a job to move the visitor one step closer to action.

02 — Target Audience

Three buyers, one journey

The landing page must speak simultaneously to three distinct visitor types. The copywriting, imagery, and CTAs must create clear pathways without alienating any segment. The unifying thread is aspiration — all three want to upgrade their life.

🏡

The Dream Buyer

Age 32–45 · First luxury home

Emotionally driven. Has been saving and visualising this moment for years. Needs validation that this agency "gets" them, not just a list of listings. Converts on emotion, not logic.

📈

The Investor

Age 40–60 · Portfolio expansion

Data-driven and time-scarce. Wants ROI indicators, market expertise, and a seamless advisory process. Converts on credibility signals and evidence of market knowledge.

🔑

The Upgrader

Age 35–50 · Selling + buying

Simultaneously needs to sell their current home and buy up. Anxious about timing and process. Converts when they believe the agency can handle both sides seamlessly.

↗

Design implication: Services section must offer three distinct paths — "Buy," "Sell," and "Invest." Each path speaks to exactly one persona. The hero search bar should have filters that let visitors self-select their intent immediately.

03 — Page Sections

Seven sections, one story

The sections below are ordered by conversion psychology, not convention. The hierarchy is intentional: every section earns the right for the next one to exist. Do not reorder without reconsidering the entire flow.

1

Hero — The Promise Attention

"Stop the scroll. Make them feel something in 3 seconds."

The hero is not a header — it is an emotional declaration. Full-viewport, cinematic. The headline should make a bold promise tied to identity, not features. Example: "Find the home that finishes your story." Beneath it, a subline grounds it: "Luxury residential properties in [city] — handpicked for how you want to live."

The hero must contain an immediately usable search bar with three filters: Location, Budget, and Property Type. This reduces friction by giving both emotional and rational visitors an instant next step. Behind the text, a slow-parallax image of a stunning property. A muted video autoplay (no audio) on desktop shows life being lived inside the space.

Cinematic full-viewport background (parallax or video)
Identity-driven headline + empathetic subline
Integrated 3-filter search bar (Location / Budget / Type)
3 property-type quick pills (House · Apartment · Villa)
Sticky transparent nav that fills on scroll
Subtle scroll-down cue (animated chevron)
Word-by-word headline reveal Parallax background on scroll Search bar fade-up (0.4s delay) Frosted-glass search overlay Sticky nav background transition
2

Trust Accelerator Bar Trust

"Kill doubt before it forms. Show proof in 5 seconds."

Immediately below the hero, before the visitor has time to formulate a skeptical thought, deliver a horizontal strip of undeniable social proof. This section should be minimal and fast — it is a credibility punch, not a content section. Use 4 animated stat counters (e.g., 2,400+ Homes Sold · 18 Years in Market · 97% Client Satisfaction · $2.4B in Sales) paired with recognisable partner/award logos in muted grayscale.

The stat numbers should animate as counters when they enter the viewport. This creates a moment of visual engagement that forces the brain to pay attention while the numbers are read.

4 animated stat counters with labels
5–7 grayscale partner / award logos
Subtle separator dividers between stats
Cream or near-white background (visual pause)
Scroll-triggered number count-up Staggered stat fade-in (60ms between each) Logo opacity from 40% → 80% on hover
3

The Problem → Our Process Interest

"Show you understand their pain. Then position yourself as the cure."

This is the section most real estate sites skip — and it is the section that creates the deepest connection with the visitor. The headline should name the real problem: "Finding your ideal home shouldn't feel like a second job." Below it, a 3-step visual process (Discover → Match → Move In) makes the agency's value proposition concrete and sequential.

The key design decision here is the split-column layout: problem stated in large, empathetic typography on the left; the 3-step process as a visual flow on the right. Each step in the process has an icon, a short title, and a single-sentence description. This is where the Upgrader persona is hooked — they see the complexity of their situation acknowledged and solved.

4

Featured Properties Desire

"Create desire. Let the product do the talking."

This is the desire engine — the section where aspiration becomes tangible. Show 6 featured properties in a magazine-style asymmetric grid: one large hero card (spanning 2 columns) and 4–5 smaller cards. Each card should show: a property photo, price, location, bedroom/bath count, and a subtle "View Details" hover reveal.

Critically, include a horizontal filter bar above the grid: All · Buy · Rent · Invest · New Build. This self-segmentation tool simultaneously serves all three personas and increases time-on-page by giving people a reason to interact. A "View All Properties" button sits below the grid to capture users ready to go deeper.

5

Expert Services Value

"Make every visitor feel there is a path specifically for them."

Three service pillars displayed as large interactive cards: Buy a Home, Sell Your Home, and Invest in Property. Each card has a full-bleed background image, bold title, 2-line description, and a "Learn More" arrow link. These are not equal-weight tiles — they should be visually weighted cards where hovering reveals more detail.

A fourth smaller card for "Home Loans & Financing" sits below or alongside, addressing the #1 practical barrier to purchasing. Including this signals the agency handles the full transaction journey, not just property finding — a key trust differentiator.

6

Client Stories Social Proof

"Let your past clients sell for you. Real words beat any copy."

A horizontally scrollable or auto-rotating testimonials section. Show 3 testimonials simultaneously on desktop, 1 on mobile. Each testimonial should include: a real client photo, first name + location, star rating, and a 2–3 sentence quote — short enough to read in full, long enough to feel genuine. Avoid generic praise; select quotes that speak to specific anxieties (e.g., "We were nervous about the timeline…").

Include a secondary row below showing property images from clients' purchased homes. This closes the loop from testimonial to tangible outcome — visitors can literally see what past clients bought, grounding the emotional proof in physical reality.

7

The Final Invitation Action

"One clear ask. No distractions. Make it impossible to say no."

The page's ultimate conversion moment. A full-width dark section with a singular focus: get the visitor to book a free consultation or submit a contact enquiry. The headline must be conversational and low-pressure: "Ready to take the first step? Let's talk." The form should ask only 3 fields: Name, Email, and "What are you looking to do?" (dropdown: Buy / Sell / Invest / Not sure yet).

Critically, offer two conversion paths side by side: a form (for the patient planner) and a phone number (for the impatient action-taker). Including both dramatically increases conversion — different psychology types need different entry points. A trust micro-copy line beneath the form ("No spam. No pressure. Just a conversation.") removes the final psychological barrier.

04 — Animation Strategy

Motion as a narrative tool

Animations on this page serve one purpose: directing attention and reinforcing the story. Every animation should feel like the page is taking the visitor on a journey. The golden rule: if an animation would still happen if you removed all content from the page, it is decorative. Remove it.

→

Performance rule: All scroll animations use Intersection Observer API — never scroll event listeners. All CSS transitions use only opacity, transform, and clip-path — never width/height/top/left (these trigger layout reflow). Target 60fps on mid-range Android. Total animation JS must be under 12kb gzipped.

Animation Where Used Trigger Duration Easing
Word-by-word text reveal Hero headline Page load 40ms per word ease-out
Parallax background shift Hero, Section 3 Scroll Continuous linear
Counter count-up Trust bar Intersection (50%) 1.4s per counter cubic-bezier(0,0,0.2,1)
Process line draw Section 3 steps Intersection (30%) 0.8s ease-in-out
Card stagger fade-up Properties, Services Intersection (20%) 0.5s + 80ms stagger ease-out
Magnetic card hover Property cards Mouse move 0.3s ease-out (JS tracked)
Testimonial auto-rotate Section 6 Timed (5s interval) 0.6s cross-fade ease-in-out
Form field sequential reveal Final CTA Intersection (40%) 0.4s + 100ms stagger ease-out

Accessibility: All animations must respect prefers-reduced-motion: reduce. When this is set, replace all motion with an instant opacity fade only.

06 — Technical Recommendations

Build for speed and scale

Technical choices should serve the business goal: fast load times, easy content management for property listings, and SEO-friendly structure. The following stack is recommended for this niche.

Framework

Next.js 14 (App Router) — SSR for SEO, ISR for property listings updates

Animations

GSAP ScrollTrigger + Framer Motion — GSAP for scroll, Framer for component transitions

Styling

Tailwind CSS + CSS custom properties — utility-first with design token overrides

CMS

Sanity.io or Contentful — structured property data with visual editor for agents

Forms & CRM

HubSpot form embed or custom API → HubSpot/Salesforce for lead routing

Images

Cloudinary with automatic WebP conversion + Next/Image lazy loading

Analytics

GA4 + Hotjar heatmaps — track scroll depth per section, form abandonment

Performance Target

LCP < 2.0s, CLS < 0.05, FID < 50ms — Core Web Vitals all green

Deployment

Vercel with Edge Network — global CDN, preview deployments, instant rollbacks

Iteration protocol: After 30 days, identify the section where the largest percentage of visitors stop scrolling (Hotjar). That section is the bottleneck. Rewrite its headline first — copy changes have 10x the conversion impact of visual changes. A/B test two variations before touching design.
